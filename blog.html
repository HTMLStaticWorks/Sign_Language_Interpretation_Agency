Visual Standards

Optimizing Your VRI Space

To ensure error-free visual translation, setting up the environment for video remote sessions requires careful planning. Here are three crucial setup tips.

Contrast & Solid Backgrounds

Use solid, neutral backgrounds (dark blue or charcoal) that contrast clearly with skin tones to make hand shapes and finger-spelling highly readable.

Wide Angle & Eye Level Placement

Mount the camera at eye level, ensuring the signing space (from head down to the waist) is fully visible without clipping.

Sufficient Frontal Lighting

Avoid strong backlighting or overhead shadows. Use ring lights or frontal lighting so facial expressions are completely clear.

Legal Standards

Federal Interpretation Mandates

Access key guidelines detailing when corporate entities, health systems, and classrooms are legally required to assign qualified interpreters.

ADA Title III Mandates Required

Mandating hospitals, hotels, and retail organizations to ensure equal communications for deaf visitors.

Section 1557 ACA Rules Healthcare

Prohibiting health programs from utilizing family members or unqualified staff for translations.
